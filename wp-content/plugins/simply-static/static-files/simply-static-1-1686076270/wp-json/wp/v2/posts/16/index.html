Many were surprised by Karim Benzema’s decision to leave. Even after that, it is difficult to accept the prediction. Real Madrid legend Karim Benzema feels the same way. The French forward said at his farewell ceremony on Tuesday that it was difficult for him to leave Real Madrid.<\/p>\n

Benzema was one of the holders of Real Madrid’s tradition for 14 years. He gave everything he needed to become a club legend. Said during his farewell, he wanted his career to end in Madrid. So why didn’t it happen? Explaining this, he said, ‘Today is a sad day. Because the club has to leave. It is difficult for me. I was working on a dream – to sign for Madrid, to end my career here. But life sometimes brings other opportunities.’<\/p>\n

The event to officially say goodbye to Benzema was held at the training ground in Madrid. In his farewell speech, Cristiano Ronaldo’s former teammate said, ‘I will always watch Madrid’s matches even if I leave. And thanks to the audience, who motivated me to play well.’<\/p>\n

At the end of his career, Benzema signed for Madrid in 2009. Before that he played for the French club Lyon. He played 648 matches for the Spanish club in these 14 years. Which is the second highest number of matches played for the club. Only Ronaldo is ahead of him. That teammate is going to be his rival in the Saudi League this time. The Portuguese star is playing for Al Nasr. And Benzema is going to Jeddah-based Al Ittihad on a three-year deal. He expressed his gratitude to Madrid President Florentino Perez during his farewell. He also said, ‘I will never forget Real Madrid. It is the best club in the world and history. But now it’s time to leave, to see another story.’<\/p>\n
